Texas Wine

Convent grapes, originally from Lyon, France, were brought to Brownsville, Texas by French Catholic nuns in 1853. Despite the demolition of the Convent of the Incarnate Word and Blessed Sacrament in 1969, the grapevines survived and were transplanted in local gardens. Today, these grapes are used by local wineries, Rubiano Vineyard and Winery, and Bonita Flats Farm and Vineyard, to produce unique wines. The story of these grapes is a testament to their durability and survival, and they continue to thrive in the Valley after over 160 years.

Wildlife in South Texas

So, like, the Lower Rio Grande Valley in Texas is this totally amazing place for wildlife! 🦋🌳 It’s got, like, over 1,200 plants, 300 butterflies, and around 700 vertebrates. And guess what? At least 520 of those are birds! 🐦✨ But wait, there’s more! 🌟 The Lower Rio Grande Valley National Wildlife Refuge is all about protecting this super important biodiversity. 🌎🌿 It’s like a wildlife corridor that follows the Rio Grande for the last 275 river miles. 🌊 It connects different pieces of land managed by private folks, nonprofits, the State of Texas, and even two other National Wildlife Refuges: Laguna Atascosa and Santa Ana. 🌿🦉 And get this—there’s this rare cat called the ocelot that lives here! 😺 But it’s endangered, so we gotta look out for it. 🚫🙀 Other cool critters include the crested caracara, Mexican bluewing butterfly, great kiskadee, red-billed pigeon, Altamira oriole, ringed kingfisher, and the green jay. 🌟🦜 So, next time you’re in the Rio Grande Valley, keep your eyes peeled for all these amazing creatures! 🌵📸 And remember, nature is like, totally awesome! 🌎🌿🌸

Brownsville has culture

In Brownsville, TX, the arts and music scene is vibrant and diverse. The city hosts events like the Brownsville Latin Jazz Festival and has venues like the Brownsville Museum of Fine Art which contribute to the cultural enrichment of the community. Charro Days is a significant annual fiesta celebrated in late February, commemorating the Mexican heritage of the area on both sides of the Rio Grande. It features parades, music, dance, and a shared heritage celebration between Brownsville and Matamoros, Tamaulipas. The event has featured grammy winning musicians and award winning actors.
